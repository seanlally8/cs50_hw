My Thing


I hate writing these things.

I hate writing these things because they require something impossible: the reduction and distillation of barely ruminated reflections on the self. On (cough) my self. The loathing I feel for this now-ubiquitious practice of branding has led me to wonder, in self-defeating bewilderment, "what's my thing?"

My. Thing.

Already, the question entails something possessive, something which is mine. But what is this "thing" which is mine? Or yours for that matter? Das ding, in the psychoanalytic tradition, reveals the contradiction at the heart of the question.

Das ding is something in me more than myself. It is, by definition, something which escapes the grasp of the self. And while we might always search after the thing, clutching our way through the dark, smiling the days away toward ruin, we can never truly possess it. Corollary: the fantasy of self must be traversed, which is to say: there's another life out there, and we're all already living it. I want to live it with you.
